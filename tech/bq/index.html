Behavioral Question

Posted on May 1, 2026

Youtuber - Matt Huang

Focus on how you think, not what you do

  • Break problem down. Ask for clarification.
  • Address the thinking process.

Demo 3 things:

  1. Leadership - how do you move the team to believe your way
  2. Resillence - biggest failure / success. This question is interesting. Although you are asked your biggest failure or success, completely opposite, but the funny thing is the response is exactly the same! It shall have such a format:
  • the backgound: you are your manager was in Samsung to solve a problem which can take 36 hours to reproduce; you have only 1 night.
  • the action: you figure out a clever way to reproduce it by fixing PC, now you can reproduce it any time you want.
  • the reaction: your customer is impressed.
  • the takeway: what you learned.
  1. Teamwork - if you have conflict with your team member, how to settle

Of course, if you are asked “failfure”, put emotional emphasis on how nervous you and your team member are, not how happy end it is.

One Story rules all:

Instead remembering one story for one question, I prefer to have a wildcard. If you asked leadeship, impress them with how proactive you are in the process; if you asked failure, emphasis on how emotional you are at the moment; and if you are asked the conflict, emphasis on how do you settle and have agreement with another team member and the consequences.
